Let’s use this table to understand changes in tenses:


Change Tense Rule Example
Continuous to Perfect Past Change "was/were" + verb-ing to "had" + past participle Was/Were writing → Had written
Continuous to Perfect Present Change "is/are" + verb-ing to "has/have" + past participle Is/Are writing → Has/Have written
Continuous to Perfect Future Change "will be" + verb-ing to "will have" + past participle Will be writing → Will have written
Perfect to Continuous Past Change "had" + past participle to "was/were" + verb-ing Had written → Was/Were writing
Perfect to Continuous Present Change "has/have" + past participle to "is/are" + verb-ing Has/Have written → Is/Are writing
Perfect to Continuous Future Change "will have" + past participle to "will be" + verb-ing Will have written → Will be writing
Continuous to Perfect Continuous Past Change "was/were" + verb-ing to "had been" + verb-ing Was writing → Had been writing
Continuous to Perfect Continuous Present Change "is/are" + verb-ing to "has/have been" + verb-ing Is/Are writing → Has/Have been writing
Continuous to Perfect Continuous Future Change "will be" + verb-ing to "will have been" + verb-ing Will be writing → Will have been writing

Words that I learnt from the story

Elusive: Difficult to find, catch, or achieve.
Sentence: The elusive cat always managed to hide before anyone could see it.

Predicament: A difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation.
Sentence: She found herself in a predicament when her car broke down in the middle of nowhere.

Undeterred: Not allowing something to stop you from doing something.
Sentence: Undeterred by the heavy rain, they continued their hike up the mountain.
